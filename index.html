Workshop Focus

Reliable world models for agents that act before they fail

World models are learned dynamical representations that simulate future visual and physical states. They can support forward and counterfactual rollouts for planning, perception, and safety-aware decision making.

This workshop focuses on two complementary roles: predictive reliability inside embodied agents, and generative safety evaluation through scalable corner-case synthesis and stress testing.

01

Predictive Reliability

Calibrated rollouts, uncertainty estimates, and physical consistency for safer planning under ambiguity.

02

Safety-Critical Generation

High-fidelity rare-event generation for systematic evaluation of autonomous systems before real-world deployment.

Call Scope

Topics of Interest

We invite research at the intersection of computer vision, robotics, machine learning, and safety-critical autonomous systems.

A

World Models for Embodied AI

Latent dynamics, simulators, action-conditioned prediction, and forward or counterfactual rollouts.

B

Uncertainty & Calibration

Predictive uncertainty, risk-aware decision making, robustness, and distribution-shift behavior.

C

Physical Consistency

Geometry, object permanence, long-horizon stability, and mitigation of physical hallucinations.

D

Corner-Case Generation

Generative and adversarial scenario synthesis for stress testing high-stakes embodied systems.

E

Evaluation Infrastructure

Benchmarks, metrics, datasets, simulator integration, and hybrid real-to-sim validation pipelines.

F

Deployment Domains

Autonomous driving, industrial automation, biomedical robotics, and human-robot collaboration.

Competition

Embodied World (Action) Model Evaluation

Phase 1

Simulation Round

Built on the RoboTwin simulator, this phase will release robotic manipulation and world-model evaluation benchmarks with 10 tasks each. Teams can iterate locally and submit to a cloud platform for standardized, auditable evaluation.

Phase 2

Real-World Round

Top teams advance to physical-robot testing. The benchmark uses teleoperated demonstrations and an API-based remote evaluation pipeline with operational support for reliable on-robot execution.

Panel Question

What does it mean for a world model to be safe in practice, and which stress tests, guardrails, and reporting standards are needed for deployment?
